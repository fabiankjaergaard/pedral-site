The Set

Both watches. One set.

Artefact is being discontinued. Final Frontier is its last expression — sold exclusively as a pair. Two calibrated counterparts. Different in tone. Identical in intent.

Terrain dial image

Terrain

Grounded. Material. Elemental.

A warm, mineral-toned dial with a fine granular texture that absorbs light rather than reflects it. The surface shifts subtly between matte and muted sheen, creating depth without gloss. Quietly confident.

Sector dial image

Sector

Technical. Precise. Controlled.

A deep, desaturated green dial with a tighter visual structure and sharper response to light. More defined. More architectural. An expression rooted in clarity and instrument-driven design.

The Watch

A complete architecture, fully resolved.

At 39.5mm, the Artefact case balances presence and restraint. Sculpted surfaces alternate between fine brushing and polished bevels, creating contrast through geometry rather than decoration.

The integrated bracelet, tapering from 22.5mm to 18mm, was designed as a continuation of the case — not an accessory to it. Hardened to 1200 HV, the entire construction is engineered to endure.

This architecture will not be produced again. Final Frontier is the last time it will exist in this form.

Kevin's Note

"Two years ago, Artefact marked a turning point for Pedral. It was the first expression of a more architectural direction — where proportion, tension, and restraint mattered more than complication. Final Frontier closes that chapter. Not because there is nothing more to add, but because nothing more needs to be. These two dials are how I always imagined Artefact ending: as a pair, resolved and complete."

Kevin Pedral, Stockholm
